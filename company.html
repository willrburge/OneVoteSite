Director


Jack Colclough

Jack Colclough, AKA Jack Cirillo, is Director for The School of Theatre and Dance at Marshall University. Jack received his MFA from the prestigious, Professional Actor Training Program (PATP) at the University of Washington in 1986 and a BS degree from the University of Idaho in 1983. Aside from his work in the classroom, Jack served as Program Director for the Marshall University School of Theatre, has had several passion projects that he created and/or helped develop over the years, not least of which is Theatre ETC!—A theatre for young audiences touring company, which he now serves as Managing Director. He was the creator and Artistic Director of The Robert Hinchman New Works Festival, and is currently a board member of The Center for Wellness in the Arts--a collaborative student support organization that provides tools of wellness for both body and mind. Jack also serves on the board for the Alchemy Theatre Troupe of Huntington, WV, and has served as a board member for the First Stage Theatre Company. During his more than 20 years with Marshall University, Jack has directed over 70 productions, most notably: Hair!, To Kill a Mockingbird, A Midsummer Night’s Dream, Dead Man Walking, A Curious Incident of The Dog in The Night-Time, and most recently, Much Ado About Nothing. As a professional actor, Jack has worked extensively on the New York City stages, as well as in many of our country’s finest regional theatres. Some of these theatres include The Tony Award-winning Roundabout Theatre Company, The Goodspeed Opera House, and Cincinnati Playhouse in the Park. He has also performed multiple times at The Repertory Theatre of St. Louis, the Colorado and North Carolina Shakespeare Festivals, and The Monomoy Theatre in Chatham, Massachusetts. He has had the good fortune to work with such notable celebrities as Gerry Orbach, Lynn Redgrave, Peter Gallagher, Jason Robert Brown, Polly Holliday, and Val Kilmer. An ardent enthusiast of all things “Shakespeare,” Jack has played or directed 24 of the 37 titles in the cannon (and is looking forward to the other 13!). He has numerous television commercials and episodic television appearances to his credit and has even performed (and danced!) with the Radio City “Rockettes” in the Radio City Christmas Spectacular.

Music Director


Michael Tobar

Michael Tobar is a multi-instrumental performer, adept in jazz, blues, rock and orchestral music. He has been involved in community theater throughout his life, having written, arranged, directed and played in countless musicals. Tobar has been an orchestral flutist for years and played in pit bands for summer stock musicals performing shows ranging from West Side Story and My Fair Lady to Man of LaMancha and Jesus Christ Superstar. He has been music director for Ohio Valley’s Summer Music Children’s Theater for over 10 years as well as directing elementary school musical productions. A retired music teacher in both colleges and public education, Tobar holds a BM in Music Education, an MM in Woodwind and Flute Performance and completed coursework in the PhD program of Interdisciplinary Arts at Ohio University.
